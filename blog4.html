What is a VPN and How Does it Work?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is a service that encrypts your internet connection and routes it through a secure server, hiding your real IP address and location.

How Does a VPN Work?

  • Your device connects to a VPN server, creating an encrypted tunnel for your data.
  • Websites and services see the VPN server's IP address, not yours.
  • This protects your privacy and can help you access content restricted by region.

When Should You Use a VPN?

  • On public Wi-Fi networks
  • When you want to hide your location
  • To bypass censorship or geo-blocks
  • For extra privacy when browsing

VPNs are a powerful tool for privacy, but choose a reputable provider and understand their policies.
