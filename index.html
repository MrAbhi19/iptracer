How IP Geolocation Works

IP geolocation uses your device's public IP address to estimate your physical location. This is done by referencing large databases that map IP address ranges to geographic regions, based on information from ISPs, network infrastructure, and user-contributed data. While generally accurate at the city or region level, it may not pinpoint your exact address and can be affected by VPNs, proxies, or mobile networks.

Use Cases for IP Lookup

  • Privacy Checks: See what information your IP reveals about your location.
  • Network Diagnostics: Troubleshoot connectivity or routing issues.
  • Website Localization: Understand how sites adapt content based on your region.
  • Security: Detect suspicious logins or access attempts from unknown locations.
  • Parental Controls: Monitor or restrict access based on location.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How accurate is IP geolocation?

Accuracy varies by provider and region. City-level accuracy is common, but exact addresses are rarely possible. VPNs and proxies can obscure your real location.

Is my privacy protected?

This tool does not store your IP or location data. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Can I use this for IPv6 addresses?

Yes! The lookup supports both IPv4 and IPv6 addresses.

Why does my location look wrong?

IP databases are not perfect and may be outdated. Mobile networks, VPNs, or corporate networks can also affect accuracy.
